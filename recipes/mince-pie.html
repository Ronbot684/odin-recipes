Traditional Mince Pies

Description

A quick treat/dessert to enjoy at any point, though usually in the colder weather. Traditionally, mince pies are filled with mincemeat, which is a combination of dried fruits, warming spices, sugar, nuts, distilled spirits, and mutton or beef suet, however most modern recipes don't use meat. Takes about 45 minutes to make.

Ingredients

  • 1 ¾ cups of all-purpose flour
  • ½ cup of confectioners' sugar, divided
  • 2 teaspoons of ground cinnamon
  • ⅔ cup of butter, softened
  • 2 tablespoons of grated orange zest
  • ¼ cup of ice water
  • ¾ cup of prepared mincemeat pie filling
  • 1 large egg, beaten

Steps

  1. Preheat the oven to 400 degrees F (200 degrees C).
  2. Sift flour, 1/4 cup confectioners' sugar, and cinnamon together in a large bowl. Use a pastry cutter or two forks to mix in butter until mixture resembles fine crumbs. Stir in orange zest.
  3. Sprinkle with ice water and gather dough into a ball. Roll out on a lightly floured surface to 1/4 inch thickness.
  4. Cut out approximately 18 (3-inch) diameter circles, and 18 (2-inch) circles, re-rolling dough as needed.
  5. Line muffin cups or mini tart tins using the 3-inch pastry circles. Fill each pastry cup with about 1 tablespoon of mincemeat filling. Top with the 2-inch pastry circles, pinching circles together to seal the edges. Brush the top of each pie with egg.
  6. Bake pies in preheated oven until tops are golden brown (about 15 to 20 minutes). Cool slightly on wire racks. Dust with remaining 1/4 cup of confectioners' sugar just before serving.
